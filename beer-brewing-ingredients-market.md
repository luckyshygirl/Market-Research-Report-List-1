<p><p>The beer brewing ingredients market comprises various types, including malt extract, adjuncts/grains, hops, beer yeast, and beer additives. Malt extract is a concentrated form of malted barley that provides fermentable sugars. Adjuncts/grains refer to additional sources of fermentable sugars, such as corn, rice, or oats, that can be added to the malt extract. Hops are flowers that add bitterness, aroma, and flavor to the beer. Beer yeast is responsible for fermentation, converting sugars into alcohol and carbon dioxide. Beer additives include various substances used to enhance flavor, color, clarity, or extend shelf life.</p></p>
