<p><p>Bottom Anti-Reflection Coatings (BARC) are used in the semiconductor industry to mitigate the reflection of light from the backside of a wafer during lithography processes. The market for BARC is divided into two types: organic and inorganic. Organic BARCs are made of carbon-based materials and offer high flexibility, easier integration, and lower cost. Inorganic BARCs, however, are composed of non-carbon-based materials and provide more robust film properties but at a higher cost. Both types are essential in optimizing the lithography process, improving overall device performance, and reducing manufacturing costs.</p></p>
