<p><p>Corrosion inhibiting admixtures are substances added to concrete or other construction materials to prevent corrosion of the embedded metal reinforcements. The market for these admixtures can be categorized into two types: organic type and inorganic type. Organic-type corrosion inhibiting admixtures are composed of organic compounds that form a protective layer on the metal surface, preventing corrosion. Inorganic-type corrosion inhibiting admixtures typically contain alkaline salts that neutralize corrosive agents and inhibit corrosion. Both types offer effective protection against corrosion, with the choice depending on specific project requirements.</p></p>
