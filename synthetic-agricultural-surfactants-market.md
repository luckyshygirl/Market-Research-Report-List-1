<p><p>Synthetic agricultural surfactants refer to chemical compounds that are added to pesticides, herbicides, and fertilizers to improve their effectiveness. These surfactants can be classified into four types: non-ionic, anionic, cationic, and amphoteric. Non-ionic surfactants are electrically neutral and widely used due to their compatibility with various products. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge and are effective in controlling water repellency. Cationic surfactants carry a positive charge and are generally used to enhance the adhesion of agrochemicals. Amphoteric surfactants can carry both positive and negative charges, making them versatile for different applications in agriculture.</p></p>
