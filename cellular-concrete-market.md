<p><p>Cellular concrete, also known as foam concrete or aerated concrete, is a lightweight construction material that consists of cement, water, and stable foam. This type of concrete offers several advantages such as enhanced thermal insulation, reduced weight, improved fire resistance, and increased workability.</p><p>The global cellular concrete market is expected to witness substantial growth during the forecast period. <p><p>The cellular concrete market includes various types based on the materials used. Gravel cellular concrete is made by mixing gravel with cementitious materials and water, providing high strength and stability. Sand cellular concrete uses sand as an aggregate, offering good thermal insulation and lightweight properties. Fly ash cellular concrete incorporates fly ash, a byproduct of coal combustion, resulting in a sustainable and environmentally friendly option. Foaming agents are added to promote the formation of air bubbles, enhancing the lightweight nature of cellular concrete. These different types cater to various construction needs and provide unique advantages.</p></p>
