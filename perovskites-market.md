<p><p>Perovskites are a versatile class of materials with various market types. Superconducting Perovskites are known for their ability to conduct electricity with zero resistance at low temperatures, promising applications in power transmission and magnetic levitation. Colossal Magnetoresistance Perovskites exhibit a strong change in electrical resistance when subjected to a magnetic field, enabling advancements in sensors and data storage. Piezoelectric Perovskites can convert mechanical stress into electrical energy, finding applications in sensors, actuators, and energy harvesting. Ferroelectric Perovskites have spontaneous electrical polarization, making them useful in electronic devices such as capacitors and memory storage.</p></p>
