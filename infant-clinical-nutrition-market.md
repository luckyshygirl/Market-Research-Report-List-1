<p><p>Infant clinical nutrition market encompasses various types of administration methods for delivering nutrition to infants. Oral administration refers to the intake of nutrition through the mouth, typically in the form of breast milk or formula. Enteral administration involves feeding through a tube that is inserted into the gastrointestinal tract. Intravenous administration, on the other hand, involves the direct delivery of nutrition into a vein, bypassing the digestive system. These methods are crucial for providing essential nutrients to infants who may have difficulty feeding orally or digesting food due to medical conditions.</p></p>
