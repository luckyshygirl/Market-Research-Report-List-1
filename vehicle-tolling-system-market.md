<p><p>A vehicle tolling system is a mechanism used to collect fees or taxes from vehicles using a specific roadway or transportation infrastructure. It typically involves the installation of toll booths or gantries equipped with advanced technologies such as radio-frequency identification (RFID), global positioning systems (GPS), or video image processing. These systems are used to automatically collect tolls from vehicles passing through designated tolling points, allowing for a more efficient and streamlined process of fee collection.</p><p>The future outlook for the vehicle tolling system market is promising. As governments continue to invest in improving transportation infrastructure, the demand for tolling systems is expected to increase. <p><p>The Vehicle Tolling System market comprises three main types: Electronic Tolling, ATMS, and UTM. Electronic Tolling enables vehicles to pay toll fees electronically, eliminating the need for cash or manual transactions. ATMS (Advanced Traffic Management System) focuses on managing traffic flow through toll plazas and monitoring vehicle movement. UTM (Unified Traffic Management) aims to integrate various technologies (such as tolling, ITS, and GIS) to provide a comprehensive solution for managing traffic and toll collection efficiently. These systems work hand in hand to ensure seamless and hassle-free toll payment and traffic management.</p></p>
