<p><p>A photosensitizer drug is a type of medication that becomes active and produces a therapeutic effect when exposed to light of a specific wavelength. These drugs are used in photodynamic therapy (PDT), a non-invasive treatment that can selectively destroy cancer cells and certain types of microbial cells. Photosensitizer drugs are administered to the patient and then activated by a light source, typically a laser, to generate reactive oxygen species that can kill the targeted cells.</p><p>The future outlook of the photosensitizer drug market is promising.
