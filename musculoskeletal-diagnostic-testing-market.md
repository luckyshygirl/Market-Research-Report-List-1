<p><p>Musculoskeletal diagnostic testing refers to various imaging techniques used to assess injuries or diseases related to the bones, muscles, and joints. X-rays capture images of the internal structures by passing low doses of radiation through the body. Dual-Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (DXA) measures bone mineral density to diagnose osteoporosis. Computed Tomography (CT) provides detailed cross-sectional images of the musculoskeletal system. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) uses radio waves and magnets to produce detailed images, particularly useful for soft tissue examination. Ultrasonography employs high-frequency sound waves to generate images. Other imaging techniques including nuclear medicine scans and arthroscopy may also be utilized.</p></p>
