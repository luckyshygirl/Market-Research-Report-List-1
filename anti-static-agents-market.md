<p><p>The market for anti-static agents can be classified into three types: ethoxylated fatty acid amines, diethanolamides, and glycerol monostearate. Ethoxylated fatty acid amines are compounds derived from fatty acids and exhibit excellent anti-static properties. Diethanolamides are chemicals formed by reacting fatty acids with diethanolamine and are widely used in various industries for their anti-static effects. Glycerol monostearate, also known as GMS, is a fatty acid ester that possesses anti-static properties and is commonly utilized as an additive in polymers, coatings, and textiles to minimize static electricity buildup.</p></p>
