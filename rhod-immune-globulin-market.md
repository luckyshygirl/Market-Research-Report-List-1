<p><p>Rho(D) immune globulin is a blood product that is used to prevent hemolytic disease of the newborn (HDN) in Rh-negative women who are pregnant or have had a miscarriage, abortion, or invasive prenatal testing. HDN occurs when an Rh-negative mother produces antibodies against the Rh-positive blood of her fetus, leading to destruction of the fetal red blood cells. Rho(D) immune globulin works by binding to and neutralizing any Rh-positive fetal red blood cells that enter the mother's bloodstream, preventing her from producing antibodies against them.</p><p>The future outlook for the Rho(D) immune globulin market appears positive. The product is widely available and is recommended by healthcare authorities for preventing HDN in Rh-negative women. Rho(D) immune globulin is typically administered as an injection and is administered at various stages of pregnancy to ensure maximum effectiveness. Additionally, it is considered a safe and well-tolerated treatment option for preventing HDN.</p><p>However, the market does face some challenges.
