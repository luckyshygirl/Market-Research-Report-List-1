<p><p>The Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML) treatment market includes various types such as targeted therapy, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, splenectomy, and stem cell transplant. Targeted therapy involves the use of medications that specifically target cancer cells, minimizing harm to healthy cells. Chemotherapy utilizes drugs to kill cancer cells throughout the body. Radiation therapy targets cancer cells with high-energy radiation beams. Splenectomy involves surgical removal of the spleen to control the excessive production of blood cells. Stem cell transplant involves replacing damaged bone marrow with healthy stem cells to restore normal blood cell production.</p></p>
