<p><p>Preclinical imaging equipment is essential for studying diseases and developing new treatments before they advance to clinical trials. Bioluminescent or fluorescent imaging involves tracking specific genes, proteins, or cells through light signals. CT or PET imaging uses X-rays or radioactive tracers to obtain detailed anatomical and functional information. Ultrasound imaging uses high-frequency sound waves to create images of internal structures. Other preclinical imaging equipment includes MRI, optical imaging, and multimodal imaging, which combines multiple techniques for more comprehensive analysis. These technologies play a crucial role in advancing medical research and facilitating the development of innovative therapies.</p></p>
