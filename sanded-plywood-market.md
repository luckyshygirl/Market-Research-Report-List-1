<p><p>The sanded plywood market is divided into three types: Class I, Class II, and Class III plywood. Class I plywood is highly durable and suitable for applications where strength and resistance to decay and delamination are crucial. Class II plywood is considered moderately durable and often used in applications that require some level of moisture resistance. Class III plywood, on the other hand, is less durable and typically used in non-structural applications such as interior furniture and decorative purposes. These classifications help customers choose the right plywood type based on their specific needs and requirements.</p></p>
