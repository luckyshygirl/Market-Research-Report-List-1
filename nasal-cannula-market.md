<p><p>The nasal cannula market comprises two types: low-flow and high-flow nasal cannulas. Low-flow nasal cannulas provide a mixture of oxygen and room air at a prescribed flow rate, typically around 1-6 liters per minute. These cannulas are commonly used in healthcare settings for patients who require continuous oxygen therapy. In contrast, high-flow nasal cannulas deliver higher rates of oxygen, ranging from 10-60 liters per minute, and are primarily used for patients with severe respiratory conditions or those undergoing procedures that necessitate high-oxygen concentration.</p></p>
