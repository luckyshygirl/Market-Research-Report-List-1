<p><p>Kinetosis drugs play a vital role in managing motion sickness and related symptoms. The market for these drugs is primarily categorized into three types: anticholinergic drugs, antihistamines, and others. Anticholinergic drugs work by blocking the action of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter responsible for inducing motion sickness. Antihistamines, on the other hand, inhibit the effects of histamine, another substance involved in motion sickness. The "others" category encompasses drugs that have different mechanisms of action, such as serotonin antagonists. Each type of drug offers various options for individuals seeking relief from kinetosis symptoms.</p></p>
