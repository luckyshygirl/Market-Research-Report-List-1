<p><p>The electric switchable glass market comprises three major types: organic dyes, conducting polymers, and metal oxides. Organic dyes are materials that change their tint when an electric current is applied. Conducting polymers are substances that possess electrical conductivity and switch their transparency with the application of voltage. Metal oxides are compounds that demonstrate variable transparency through nanostructure engineering. All these types of switchable glass offer the ability to control the opacity or transparency of glass, providing enhanced privacy, energy efficiency, and aesthetics in various applications.</p></p>
