Indirect fillings, on the other hand, are created outside the mouth and then placed into the tooth cavity, requiring at least two dental visits. Both types of bioactive fillings offer the advantage of enhanced dental health and are widely used in dentistry for repairing and restoring tooth structure.</p></p>
