<p><p>Dicamba herbicide is commonly used in agriculture to control broadleaf weeds in various crops. It has two main application methods: pre-emergence and post-emergence. Pre-emergence application refers to the application of dicamba herbicide before the target weeds germinate or emerge from the soil. This helps prevent weed establishment and growth alongside the crop plants. Post-emergence application involves using dicamba herbicide on actively growing weeds that have already emerged. This method targets and eliminates existing weeds, allowing the crop to grow without competition for nutrients and resources. Both pre-emergence and post-emergence applications play crucial roles in maintaining crop quality and maximizing yields.</p></p>
