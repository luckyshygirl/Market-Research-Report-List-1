<p><p>Worm gears and worm mechanisms are mechanical power transmission components used in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, mining, and food processing. A worm gear consists of a worm screw and a worm wheel. The worm screw, often referred to as a worm, is a threaded cylindrical shaft, while the worm wheel is a toothed wheel that meshes with the worm screw. This configuration enables the worm gear to transmit motion between non-parallel shafts and provides high gear ratio capabilities.</p><p>The market for worm gears and worm mechanisms is witnessing steady growth due to their extensive use in industries requiring smooth and efficient power transmission. Key factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for automation in industries, the growing need for highly precise motion control in machinery, and the rising adoption of worm gears in vehicles for steering systems.
