<p><p>Petroleum resins are widely used in various industries, including adhesives, coatings, and rubber manufacturing. There are different types of petroleum resins available in the market:</p><p>1. C5 resins: These resins are derived from the cracking of petroleum naphtha. They offer excellent adhesive properties, good heat resistance, and low odor characteristics.</p><p>2. C9 resins: These resins are obtained from the cracking of heavy residue from petroleum distillation. They provide good tack, improved heat and chemical resistance, and are commonly used in hot-melt adhesives and coatings.</p><p>3. C5/C9 resins: These resins are a blend of both C5 and C9 resins, offering a balance of properties from both types.</p><p>4. Hydrogenated resins: These resins are obtained by hydrogenation of petroleum resins, resulting in improved color stability, thermal stability, and resistance to oxidation.</p><p>Overall, these different types of petroleum resins cater to the diverse needs of various industries and applications.</p></p>
