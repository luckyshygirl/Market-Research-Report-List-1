<p><p>AC Dynamometers are used to measure the torque and power output of engines. Air Cooled Dynamometers use ambient air for cooling, making them suitable for environments with sufficient ventilation. These dynamometers are smaller and more portable but have lower power capacity. Water Cooled Dynamometers utilize water as a coolant, allowing for higher power capacities. They are larger and require a water supply for cooling, making them more suitable for fixed installations. Both types offer accurate and reliable torque and power measurements, with the choice depending on the specific application and cooling requirements.</p></p>
