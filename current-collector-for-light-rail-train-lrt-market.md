<p><p>The current collector is an important component of a light rail train (LRT) as it collects electricity from the overhead wire to power the train. There are three types of market for current collectors: top-running, bottom-running, and side-running. In the top-running market, the collector is located on the top of the train and glides along the overhead wire. In the bottom-running market, the collector is placed underneath the train and makes contact with the wire from below. In the side-running market, the collector is positioned at the side of the train and connects to the wire horizontally.</p></p>
