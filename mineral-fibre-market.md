<p><p>The mineral fibre market consists of various types such as glasswool, rockwool, slagwool, glass filaments, and ceramic fibres. Glasswool is made by melting glass and spinning it into fibres. Rockwool is manufactured from volcanic rock using a similar process. Slagwool is produced from blast furnace slag. Glass filaments are formed by drawing molten glass into thin strands. Ceramic fibres, on the other hand, are made from a mixture of alumina and silica. These different types of mineral fibres offer a range of applications in industries such as construction, automotive, and insulation.</p></p>
