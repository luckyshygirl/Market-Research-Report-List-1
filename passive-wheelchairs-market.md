<p><p>Passive wheelchairs are mobility devices designed for individuals who have limited or no upper body strength. Unlike power wheelchairs that require an external power source or manual wheelchairs that need to be self-propelled, passive wheelchairs are propelled by an attendant or caregiver. <p><p>Passive wheelchairs are categorized into Type I and Type II markets based on their characteristics. Type I passive wheelchairs are designed for general use and are typically self-propelled by the user. These wheelchairs provide basic mobility assistance and are suitable for individuals with limited mobility. Type II passive wheelchairs, on the other hand, are more advanced and often motorized, allowing for enhanced maneuverability and control. They are usually equipped with additional features such as adjustable seating, power recline, and tilt functions, making them suitable for individuals with more complex mobility needs.</p></p>
