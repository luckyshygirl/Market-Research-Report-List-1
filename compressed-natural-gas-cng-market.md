<p><p>Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) can be derived from different sources, including associated gas, non-associated gas, and unconventional methods. Associated gas is found alongside crude oil during extraction and requires minimal processing before being compressed. Non-associated gas is extracted separately without any accompanying crude oil. Unconventional methods involve extracting CNG from unconventional reservoirs like shale formations through techniques like hydraulic fracturing. These three market types represent different sources for obtaining CNG, with each requiring specific extraction and processing methods.</p></p>
