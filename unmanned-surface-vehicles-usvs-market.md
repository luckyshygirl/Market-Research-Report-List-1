<p><p>Unmanned Surface Vehicles (USVs) are autonomous or remotely operated vehicles that are designed to operate on the surface of the water without a crew onboard. The USV market can be categorized into two types: wave-powered and propeller-driven. Wave-powered USVs utilize the energy generated by the waves to propel themselves, making them environmentally friendly and cost-effective. On the other hand, propeller-driven USVs are powered by electric motors or internal combustion engines, providing higher speed and maneuverability. These different market types cater to various industries and applications, such as oceanographic research, surveillance, and maritime security.</p></p>
