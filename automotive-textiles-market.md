<p><p>The automotive textiles market can be divided into three main types: woven, nonwoven, and composites. Woven textiles are made by interlacing yarns in a crisscross pattern, resulting in a strong and durable material for car interiors, seat covers, and carpets. Nonwoven textiles are produced by bonding fibers together using heat or chemicals, creating a versatile and cost-effective fabric used for flooring, trunk liners, and insulation. Composites combine different materials like fibers, fabrics, and resins to create lightweight and high-strength components, commonly used in car body parts, bumpers, and panels.</p></p>
