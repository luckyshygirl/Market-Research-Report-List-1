<p><p>Extracorporeal circuit cannulae are medical devices used during procedures such as cardiopulmonary bypass surgeries. Arterial cannulae are placed into a patient's artery to redirect blood flow from the heart to the bypass pump. Venous cannulae, on the other hand, are inserted into a patient's vein to return the blood from the bypass pump back to the body. These cannulae are integral components of the extracorporeal circuit, enabling circulation of blood during surgical procedures, ensuring oxygenation, and maintaining the patient's vital functions.</p></p>
